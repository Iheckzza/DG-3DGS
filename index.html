DG-3DGS: Dynamic Growing 3D Gaussian Splatting for Monocular Laparoscopic Sequential Scene Reconstruction

Ziang Zhanga, Hong Songb,*, Jingfan Fanc,*, Tianyu Fua, Danni Aic, Deqaing Xiaoc,*, Jian Yang


aThe School of Medical Technology, Beijing Institute of Technology, Beijing 100081, China
bThe School of Computer Science&Technology, Beijing Institute of Technology, Beijing 100081, China
cThe School of Optics and Photonics, Beijing Institute of Technology, Beijing 100081, China
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

Monocular laparoscopic videos scene reconstruction is essential for visualization enhancement of surgical applications in computer-assisted surgery. However, constrained by the limited range of viewpoints and narrow field of view inherent in laparoscopic, currently most exiting methods like Neural Radiance Field (NeRF) or 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) rely heavily on high-accurate pre-processed camera poses as well as initial reconstruction data, which imposes stringent requirements on monocular endoscopic video quality and navigation equipment. To relax these limitations, in this paper we utilize the continuity of the input video stream and the feature correspondences of interframes to achieve novel view synthesis, and propose a novel Dynamic Growing 3D Gaussian splatting (DG-3DGS) architecture to progressively grow the gaussian set by taking continuous monocular laparoscopic frames and reconstruct the laparoscopic scene. 1) Based on dense feature matching prior, we present a 2D-3D displacement vector field projecting optimization method to estimate the small pose transformation between sequential interframes. 2) Grounded in the initial gaussian set generated from the first frame and pose transformation, we propose a dynamic gaussian set growing strategy to generate new view of transformed gaussian set with frames flow. 3) Integrated with 1 and 2, we design a novel sequential reconstruction architecture under monocular laparoscopic scene and generate high-quality gaussian reconstructed model. The evaluation is conducted on two public laparoscopic datasets: Scared and C3VD. The experimental results show the proposed architecture outperforms state-of-the-art methods in PSRN, SSIM, LIPIS and qualitative comparison. 
